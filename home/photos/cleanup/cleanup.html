May 19th, 2001

 They had the fire started not long after sunrise.

Even the littlest islanders helped clean up the beach.

Volunteers brought brush down for those who couldn't.

At one point, we had five trucks waiting to unload - a traffic jam at North Beach!

The neat loading technique...

and the not-so-neat loading technique.

Suzie keeping the pile tidy. Nick used his backhoe to keep the fire the right size.

We lost count after more than 40 truckloads
 full of brush!

Gary tuned in the Mariners game so the volunteers wouldn't
miss it.

With one community fire, we eliminated the need for dozens of small fires...smart idea!

The Boosters baked
a turkey for
fresh deli sandwiches with all the fixins'.

There was also chili, chips, carrot cake, tons of cookies, coffee & lemonade.

Always working - Mary Turpin tended the fire so others could eat lunch.

Check out Kathy's three-marshmallow fork!

Some of the crew getting ready to add the last wreath to the fire.

A heck of a pile of ashes! Volunteers monitored the coals
into the night.
